Test 1: The Topology of a Tensegrity Unit

  1. Assemble one tensegrity unit with 3 bars and one with 4 bars. Follow the next steps for the prism with 3 bars.

  2. Label the bars and strings. Make sure the bars are labeled sequentially.

  3. On your lab notebook, draw three parallel bars. Identify the bars with the numbering you have on the actual prism.

  4. Looking at the prism, draw straight lines representing the strings. If a string goes over a bar or another string just indicate the right connection. You have just produced a connectivity diagram for a tensegrity unit.

  5. Answer the discussion question and then return to item (b) for the 4-bar prism.

Discussion

  1. Can you produce a similar diagram for the 4-bar unit (without looking at the unit itself).

Test 2: The Topology of a Two Stage Tensegrity Tower

  1. Request an alredy built two-stage tensegrity tower and draw its connectivity diagram.

  2. Use the connectivity diagram you obtained in the previous item to build a two-stage tensegrity tower with three bars on each stage.

Discussion

  1. Can you draw the connectivity diagram of a two-stage tower with four bars on each stage.
  2. Can you draw the connectivity diagram of a three-stage tower with three bars on each stage.

Test 3: To Infinity and Beyond

  1. Use the connectivity diagram you obtained in the previous discussion to build a two-stage tensegrity tower with fours bars on each stage.

  2. Use the connectivity diagram you obtained in the previous discussion to build a three-stage tensegrity tower with three bars on each stage.

Discussion

  1. How far can you take this process?
  2. What happens if you close the top and bottom as well as the sides of the planar topology?
