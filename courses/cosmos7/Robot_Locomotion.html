Experimenting with the MIP

Write python code to perform the following tasks with your wheeled MIP:

  1. Set the signal pwm1 = 50% and pmw2 = 50% during 3 seconds. Plot the response of both encoder1 and encoder2. Plot also the encoder velocities.
  2. Repeat the experiment with pwm1 = 25% and pmw2 = 25%
  3. Repeat the experiment with pwm1 = 50% and pmw2 = 25%
  4. Repeat the experiment with pwm1 = 50% and pmw2 = 0%
  5. Repeat the experiment with pwm1 = 50% and pmw2 = -25%
  6. Repeat the experiment with pwm1 = 50% and pmw2 = -50%

Questions

  1. What is the relationship between the value of the pwm signals and the values of encoders in the first two experiments?
  2. What is the relationship between the value of the pwm signals and the values of the encoder velocities in the first two experiments?
  3. What is the geometry of the trajectory of the robot in each case?
  4. Is there any direction in which the robot cannot move toward?
  5. What is the relation between the value and the sign of the pwm signals on the movement of the robot?

How does a two-wheeled robot move?

If a two-wheeled robot is moving in a circle of radius <math>R</math> and <math>r</math> is the distance between the center of the robot axle and each wheel then

<math>
 v_r = \omega \, (R + \ell/2), \quad 
 v_l = \omega \, (R - \ell/2)

</math>

where <math>v_l</math> is the velocity of the left wheel, and <math>v_r</math> is the velocity of the right wheel.

Questions

  1. Can you show that:
    <math>R = \frac{\ell}{2} \frac{v_r + v_l}{v_r - v_l}, \quad \omega = \frac{v_r - v_l}{\ell} </math>
  2. What does the quantity:
    <math>v = \frac{v_r + v_l}{2}</math>
    correspond to?
  3. What happens when:
    • <math>v_r > v_l > 0</math>
    • <math>0 < v_r < v_l</math>
    • <math>v_r < 0 < v_l</math>
    • <math>v_r = v_l</math>
    • <math>v_r = - v_l</math>
    • <math>v_r = 0</math> or <math>v_l = 0</math>
  4. What is the effect of <math>\ell</math> on the movement of the robot?
  5. Why would you choose a robot with a little <math>\ell</math>?
  6. Why would you choose a robot with a big <math>\ell</math>?
  7. How different is the movement of your robot from that of a regular car?

How does the speed of the wheels affect the robot trajectory?

Let <math>r</math> be the radius of both left and right wheels and <math>\omega_l</math> and <math>\omega_r</math> be the wheels' angular speed.

Questions

  1. Can you show that:
    <math>R = \frac{\ell}{2} \frac{\omega_r + \omega_l}{\omega_r - \omega_l}, \quad \omega = \frac{r}{\ell} (\omega_r - \omega_l) </math>
  2. Can you show that:
    <math>v = \frac{r}{2} (\omega_r + \omega_l) </math>
  3. What happens when:
    • <math>\omega_r = \omega_l</math>
    • <math>\omega_r = - \omega_l</math>
    • <math>\omega_r = 0</math> or <math>\omega_l = 0</math>

Repeat the experiments with the MIP you performed above, this time calculating and plotting the radius <math>R</math> and the angular velocity <math>\omega</math>. Calculate also the curvature <math>\kappa = R^{-1}</math>.
