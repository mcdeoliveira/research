Balancing a Stick

In order to understand how class-<math>1</math> tensegrity structures can be put in stable equilibrium this lab develops activities that will help you understand the fundamentals of such equilibrium by working with a single stick.

Balancing a stick with 3 strings

  1. Attach one extra string to one end of a tensegritoy bar as shown below. Leave the other end with just one string attached.

  2. Pull the loose ends of the strings and observe the orientation of the bar.

Discussion

  1. Describe the orientation of the bar relative to the strings.
  2. Can you find a configuration in which the angles between the bar and both of the two tensioned strings in one end are less then 90<math>^\mathrm{o}</math>?
  3. What direction should the sum of the forces of the two strings in the same end have?

Balancing a stick with 4 strings

  1. Attach one extra string to the other end of the tensegritoy bar as shown in Test 2. Now both ends should have two strings attached.

  2. Pull the loose ends of all the strings and observe the orientation of the bar.

  3. Find a configuration in which the angles between the bar and the tensioned strings in both ends is less then 90<math>^\mathrm{o}</math>.

Discussion

  1. Describe the orientation of the bar relative to the strings.
  2. Can you find a configuration in which the bar and the two tensioned strings are not in the same plane?
  3. What direction should the sum of the forces of the strings in each end have?
