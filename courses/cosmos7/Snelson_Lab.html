Snelson Lab

Background

In this lab we will build the simplest 3D tensegrity structure: the snelson prism. The Tensegritoy kits will be used for the construction. The kits contain three basic building blocks: the tensegrity sticks, the elastic strings with appropriate ends for attachment, and rubber caps to seal the tensegrity joints. Take some time to familiarize yourself with how the pieces interact before attempting this lab.

Building a 3-Bar Regular Snelson Prism

These steps and pictures are meant to guide you through the process of constructing a snelson prism. The steps involved are the same regardless of the aspect ratio of the prism you wish to construct.

  1. For one snelson prism, you will need: 3 bars, 6 strings, and 6 rubber caps. Gather these materials.

  2. Prepare the bars. Place the metal ends of two strings in the corresponding holes of the bars as is shown below.

  3. While keeping the ends of the stings in the corresponding holes, route the end of each string, that is closest the the end of the bar, over the top of the bar and through the groove to the opposite side.

  4. Now, route the other end of each string through the groove of the closest end of the bar. You should now have two equally taut strings, one on each side of the bar.
